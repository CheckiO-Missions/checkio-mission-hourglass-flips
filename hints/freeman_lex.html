The base cases of recursion are when t equals 0 or when exactly t minutes remain in some upper chamber (no flips are needed), or when t is smaller than the smallest time remaining in the upper chamber of any hourglass (no solution is possible). Otherwise, update all hourglasses to their new states after m minutes, and loop through all possible subsets of glasses to flip. For each such subset, recursively construct the optimal sequence of moves to measure the remaining t - m minutes, and combine these moves to the best solution.
